# Cost-Benefit Trade-offs of Metabolite Exchange in Obligately Dependent Cross-Feeding Microbial Pairs

## Thesis Project by Ayaan Dutt, BSc (Hons.) Physics, Ashoka University

Under the guidance of Prof. Sandeep Krishna (NCBS) and Prof. Gautam Menon (Ashoka University)

### Abstract

Microbial communities frequently engage in metabolic cross-feeding, exchanging diffusible metabolites that can stabilize interactions and enhance fitness. Although obligate cross-feeding is often associated with the exchange of biosynthetically costly metabolites, it remains unclear whether such interactions can yield a net fitness advantage when partner species must invest energy to produce metabolites they themselves do not consume. Building on the network-based framework of Goyal and Krishna (2022), I aim to construct large ensembles of in silico metabolic networks derived from the KEGG reaction repertoire to investigate the conditions under which costly obligate cross-feeding can outperform autonomous metabolism. I assemble minimal independent networks capable of synthesizing essential biomass precursors and extend this construction to obligately dependent network pairs that exchange intermediate metabolites, including those whose exchange imposes a fitness cost. I then plan to estimate metabolic yields using both stoichiometry-based fluxallocation schemes and alternative constraint-based methods such as flux balance analysis (FBA) to ensure robustness. By comparing the distributions of biomass and energy yields across autonomous and cross-feeding networks, I hope to identify the structural and biochemical features that enable costly metabolite exchange to confer a net benefit.
